5 Core Demands

THE MANIFESTO.

Five non-negotiable demands. Not suggestions. Not requests. Demands. Click each card to read more.

1
📋

EXAM LEAKS =
NATIONAL BETRAYAL

Students prepare for years. One corrupt official destroys it overnight for a few thousand rupees."But beta, it happens everywhere." — No. It shouldn't happen anywhere.

  • Non-bailable offences for paper leak perpetrators
  • Lifetime bans for every official involved
  • Fast-track courts for exam fraud
  • End-to-end transparent examination systems
2
🛡️

SAFE CAMPUSES.
SAFE FUTURES.

No girl should plan her route home before her career."Why were you out late?" — Because she has a life. That's why.

  • Mandatory campus safety audits
  • Proper lighting, CCTV, and safe transport
  • 24/7 emergency helplines on every campus
  • Zero-tolerance policy — actual enforcement
3
⚖️

EQUAL RIGHTS.
EQUAL RESPECT.

Equality for all. Women's rights matter. Men's mental health matters. Both can be true simultaneously.Revolutionary concept: humans deserve dignity.

  • Equal pay and opportunity — no asterisks
  • Men's mental health destigmatized, now
  • Fast justice for gender-based crimes
  • 33% women in leadership — minimum
4
🚫

NO VIP CULTURE.
NO FREEBIE POLITICS.

Leadership is service. Not a red beacon, not a fleet of cars, not your face on every overpass.Your sarkari bungalow is not a tourist attraction.

  • Jobs over slogans, every single time
  • Skills over handouts — we're not pets
  • Accountability over privilege
  • Every public servant answers to the public
5
🏛️

JUSTICE SHOULD NOT DEPEND ON POWER.

Your surname should not determine your sentence. Lady Justice is supposed to be blind — not selectively blind when it's convenient."Don't you know who my father is?" — Irrelevant. Completely irrelevant.

  • Legally mandated case resolution timelines — no infinite delays
  • Zero political interference in investigations
  • Equal quality legal aid for every Indian
  • Elected officials under the exact same law as everyone else

WHO IS YIP?

YIP was not born in boardrooms by career politicians. It was born in frustration — in exam halls, college corridors, and late-night group chats where everyone was venting about the same broken things.

We are students, graduates, first-time voters, and everyday citizens who looked at the state of things and said: no thanks, we'll fix it ourselves.

Other parties talk TO youth. YIP is BUILT BY youth.

"We didn't choose to make a political movement. We just ran out of other options. The system wasn't listening — so we decided to become the system."
